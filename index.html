Ned Stark

House Stark

Eddard "Ned" Stark is the honorable Lord of Winterfell and head of House Stark. He is a devoted father an husband, deeply committed to the values of duty and loyalty. Called to serve as Hand of the King to his friend, Robert Baratheon, Ned faces the treacherous politics of King’s Landing, uncovering secrets that put him and his family in grave danger.

Jon Snow

House Stark

Jon Snow, born Aegon Targaryen, is the son of Lyanna Stark and Rhaegar Targaryen, the late Prince of Dragonstone. From infancy, Jon is presented as the bastard son of Lord Eddard Stark, Lyanna's brother, and raised alongside Eddard's lawful children at Winterfell. Jon's true parentage is kept secret from everyone, including Jon himself, in order to protect him from those that sought the complete annihilation of House Targaryen.

Jaime Lannister

House Lannister

Ser Jaime Lannister, known as the Kingslayer, is the eldest son of Tywin Lannister and a member of the Kingsguard. Renowned for his skill in combat and feared for breaking his oath to kill the Mad King, Jaime’s reputation precedes him. Loyal to his sister Cersei, he finds his loyalty tested and his identity challenged through his encounters with war and loss.

Tyrion Lannister

House Lannister

Tyrion Lannister, often called "The Imp" due to his small stature, is the witty and sharp-tongued youngest son of Tywin Lannister. Despite his family’s contempt, Tyrion’s intelligence and resourcefulness make him a valuable advisor in the politically charged courts of Westeros. With his quick wit and keen understanding of power, Tyrion navigates treachery and personal trials to prove his worth.

Cersei Lannister

House Lannister

Cersei Lannister, the ambitious and fiercely protective daughter of Tywin Lannister, is the Queen of the Seven Kingdoms and the mother of three children. Determined to secure her family’s legacy and eliminate any threats to her power, Cersei’s actions often plunge her into schemes that alienate both allies and enemies. Her pursuit of dominance has drastic consequences for the realm.

Joffrey Baratheon

House Lanniste

Joffrey Baratheon, often believed to be the son of Robert Baratheon but secretly fathered by Jaime Lannister, ascends to the Iron Throne with a cruel disposition. Known for his sadistic nature and impulsive decisions, Joffrey’s reign is marked by fear and brutality, earning him the ire of both his subjects and his own family.

Daenerys Targaryen

House Targaryen

Daenerys Targaryen, the last surviving child of King Aerys II, grows up in exile across the Narrow Sea. Initially timid, she finds inner strength through hardship and her bond with her dragons. Driven by a vision of reclaiming her family’s throne, Daenerys becomes a powerful leader, inspiring fierce loyalty in her followers as she fights to end tyranny and bring justice.

Arya Stark

House Stark

Arya Stark, the fiercely independent youngest daughter of Eddard and Catelyn Stark, defies traditional roles expected of noble ladies. Forced to flee after her father’s death, Arya trains as an assassin, fueled by a relentless desire to avenge her family. Her journey takes her across Westeros and beyond, honing her skills and forging her own path through a dangerous world.

Sansa Stark

House Stark

Sansa Stark, the eldest daughter of Eddard and Catelyn Stark, initially dreams of courtly romance and becoming a queen. Through traumatic experiences and betrayal, she learns the harsh realities of political life. With resilience and newfound cunning, Sansa transforms from a naïve girl into a skilled and poised leader, reclaiming her place within the North.
